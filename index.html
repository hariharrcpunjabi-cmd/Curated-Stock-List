How the stop-loss works

  1. Structural exit (primary). A weekly close below the rising 30-week moving average means Stage 2 is over — exit. This stop trails up on its own as the 30-week MA climbs. Confirm it on your chart each weekend; it isn't a fixed number.
  2. Protective stop (tactical). Because the 30-week MA can sit far below a fresh entry, set a hard floor at 8% below entry to cap the initial drawdown. Slider above adjusts it live.
  3. Trail it. Move the stop to breakeven once the stock is up ~8% from entry. After that, trail the protective stop 8% below the highest weekly close reached.
  4. Position size. Enter your rupee risk per trade above and each card shows suggested quantity: qty = risk ÷ (entry − stop). Keeps every position risking the same amount regardless of price.
